# Arizona Lawyer Discipline — Procedure

**Authority:** Rules of the Arizona Supreme Court governing lawyer discipline (the discipline rules in the Rule 46–80 range). Official text: azcourts.gov (Attorney Discipline / Lawyer Regulation) and Westlaw Arizona Court Rules. **This file describes the structure and process; confirm exact rule numbers and verbatim text at the official source before citing.**

## Structure of the Arizona discipline system (post-2011 reforms)

Arizona centralized attorney discipline under the Supreme Court with a dedicated adjudicator:

- **State Bar of Arizona — Office of Bar Counsel / Attorney Discipline.** Receives charges of misconduct, investigates, and prosecutes disciplinary matters. Intake begins with a **charge of misconduct** (the State Bar provides an online Charge of Misconduct form).
- **Attorney Discipline Probable Cause Committee.** Reviews matters and makes **probable cause** determinations authorizing formal complaints.
- **Presiding Disciplinary Judge (PDJ).** A judicial officer who presides over formal disciplinary proceedings, conducts hearings, and (with a hearing panel) issues decisions; also handles interim suspensions, reciprocal discipline, and related matters.
- **Hearing Panels.** Typically the PDJ sitting with a volunteer lawyer and a volunteer public (nonlawyer) member for contested formal hearings.
- **Disciplinary Clerk.** Maintains the record and dockets disciplinary proceedings.
- **Arizona Supreme Court.** Exercises ultimate authority over lawyer discipline; reviews PDJ/hearing-panel decisions.

## Process (overview)
1. **Charge / intake** → screening by Bar Counsel.
2. **Investigation** → dismissal, diversion, or referral.
3. **Probable cause** determination by the Probable Cause Committee.
4. **Formal complaint** filed before the PDJ.
5. **Answer, discovery, hearing** before the PDJ / hearing panel.
6. **Decision and sanction**, with the **ABA Standards for Imposing Lawyer Sanctions** guiding the sanction (disbarment, suspension, reprimand, admonition, probation, restitution, costs).
7. **Review** by the Arizona Supreme Court.

## Lesser dispositions
- **Diversion / Alternative Disposition** for lesser misconduct (e.g., fee arbitration, the Member Assistance Program, the State Bar's Continuing Legal Education or trust-account programs, ethics enhancement).
- **Probation** with terms (e.g., LOMAP/trust-account oversight, MAP for substance/mental-health issues).

## Related
- **Confidentiality** of disciplinary matters typically attaches until a determination of probable cause (confirm the current rule).
- **Reinstatement** after suspension/disbarment is governed by the Supreme Court rules (confirm rule numbers; reinstatement after a serious sanction generally requires a formal application and, in some cases, proof of rehabilitation).
- **Interim suspension** is available for lawyers posing a substantial threat of serious harm.
- **Client Protection Fund** reimburses clients harmed by lawyer dishonesty (administered by the State Bar).
