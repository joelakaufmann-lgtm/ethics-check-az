# Arizona Supreme Court Rules — Admission, Duties, Trust Accounts/IOLTA, MCLE

**Authority:** Rules of the Arizona Supreme Court, Rules 31 et seq. (the rules regulating the practice of law). Official text: the Arizona Judicial Branch / Arizona Supreme Court rules (azcourts.gov) and Westlaw Arizona Court Rules. **This file summarizes the framework and reproduces verified rule text where available; confirm exact current text and numbering at the official source before quoting.** (The State Bar's ViewRule endpoint covers only the ER Ethical Rules, not these Supreme Court Rules.)

## Key rules (framework)

- **Rule 31 — Regulation of the Practice of Law; Unauthorized Practice.** Defines the practice of law and who may practice; foundation of UPL enforcement. **Rule 31.1** sets who is "authorized to practice law in this jurisdiction," including (per the 2021 reforms) lawyers, **Legal Paraprofessionals (LPs)**, and entities operating as **Alternative Business Structures (ABS)**. (ER 1.0(p)–(q) cross-reference Rule 31.1(a).) *Verify current subparts.*
- **Rule 32 — State Bar of Arizona.** Organization, membership, and governance of the State Bar (an arm of the Supreme Court).
- **Rule 33 / 33.1 — Membership; ABS registration/licensing.** Active/inactive status; the licensing of Alternative Business Structures. *Verify.*
- **Admission (bar exam, character & fitness).** Admission to practice, the bar examination, and character-and-fitness requirements are governed by the Supreme Court's admission rules (historically Rules 31–37; the Committee on Examinations and the Committee on Character and Fitness). *Confirm the current admission-rule numbers at the official source.*
- **In-house counsel and pro hac vice.** Arizona provides for registration of in-house counsel and for **pro hac vice** admission of out-of-state lawyers. *Confirm the current rule numbers (pro hac vice is administered under the Supreme Court rules; verify the exact rule before citing).*
- **Rule 41 — Duties and Obligations of Members.** Enumerates the duties of State Bar members (e.g., to support the constitution/laws, maintain respect for courts, employ means consistent with truth, maintain client confidences, etc.). *Quote the verbatim list from the official source.*
- **Rule 42 — Arizona Rules of Professional Conduct (the ERs).** See `rpc.md`.
- **Rule 45 — Mandatory Continuing Legal Education (MCLE).** Arizona's MCLE requirement and affidavit. (Rule 45 was amended effective Jan. 1, 2025; MCLE Regulations effective June 27, 2022.)

## Rule 43 — Trust Accounts; IOLTA (verified text, as quoted in Ariz. Ethics Op. 09-03)

Rule 43 governs lawyer trust accounts. Trust-account **location** is controlled by ER 1.15(a). Key provisions:

- **Rule 43(a) — Duty to Deposit Client Funds.** "Funds belonging in whole or in part to a client or third person in connection with a representation shall be kept separate and apart from the lawyer's personal and business accounts. All such funds shall be deposited into one or more trust accounts that are labeled as such. The location of the trust account shall be controlled by the provisions of ER 1.15(a). No trust account required by this rule may have overdraft protection…."
- **Rule 43(f)(2) — Pooled (IOLTA) Trust Account.** A lawyer/firm receiving client funds "shall maintain a pooled interest-bearing or dividend-earning trust account for deposit of client funds unless the funds are expected to earn net income for the client in excess of the costs incurred to secure such income. The interest or dividends accruing on this account, net of any reasonable service or other charges or fees… shall be paid by the financial institution… to the **Arizona Foundation for Legal Services and Education**," to be used solely for: administrative costs of the IOLTA program; programs assisting delivery of legal services to the poor; law-related education; studies/programs to improve the administration of justice; and a reasonable reserve.
- **Rule 43(f)(3)** — Alternative: a separate interest-bearing account for the particular client (interest paid to the client), or a pooled account with subaccounting that computes and pays each client's interest to the client.
- **Rule 43(g) — Authorized Financial Institutions.** A trust account may be maintained only at an institution that has filed an agreement with the State Bar to comply with the rules, including the **duty to report to chief bar counsel any properly payable instrument presented against a trust account containing insufficient funds** (overdraft reporting). The State Bar and Foundation annually publish a list of approved institutions.
- **Rule 43(j) — Applicability.** "Every lawyer admitted to practice law in Arizona shall comply with the provisions of this rule regarding funds received, disbursed or held in Arizona, and funds received, disbursed or held on behalf of an Arizona client or a third person in connection with the representation of an Arizona client."

*Rule 43 also addresses trust-account recordkeeping and verification; ER 1.15 Comment cross-references "Supreme Court Rules 43(i) and 44." Confirm subparts (b)–(e), (h)–(i) and Rule 44 at the official source.*

- **Rule 44 — Trust Accounts; Interest Thereon (IOLTA mechanics).** Companion to Rule 43 on interest/earnings remittance. *Verify current text.*

## IOLTA administrator

**Arizona Foundation for Legal Services & Education (AZFLSE)** administers the IOLTA program and receives pooled-account interest under Rule 43(f)(2).
